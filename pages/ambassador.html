Become an Ambassador

Join the seedsnitch ambassador program and become a superhero sidekick that help peers turn ideas into startups and also earn exciting rewards for each referral..

What is it about?

Welcome to the Seedsnitch Campus Ambassador Program, where we turn boring college students into cool startup ambassadors. As a campus ambassador, you'll have the opportunity to spread the word about Seedsnitch and all the awesome resources we offer to help college students turn their ideas into successful businesses. Imagine being able to say, "I'm a startup ambassador," at parties or on a first date. It's a conversation starter, trust us. But it's not just about impressing your friends; it's also about making a real impact on your campus. You'll have the opportunity to help identify and recruit the next generation of college startup founders, and in return, you'll get to be part of a community of like-minded individuals who are also on the journey of building their own startups, gain valuable experience in the startup world, and earn some extra money (you were finding that, weren't you?). Think of it as a referral program: the more members you bring in, the more rewards you earn.

How does this work?

You apply for the program, and if we love your application (and let's face it, who wouldn't love you? ), we'll give you a call and invite you to join our squad. Your responsibilities as a campus ambassador are simple: Make your peers aware of Seedsnitch and find those hidden gems: the students on your campus who have a burning passion to start a business but just need a little push. And that's where we come in. We're here to be the support system for all those college students who have big dreams and the drive to make them a reality. We're not in it for the money; we're in it for the passion. If you bring us a startup idea that has potential and gets funded, you'll get a cut of the deal. So, the more potential ideas you bring in, the more money you can make. But you might be thinking, "How do I identify a potential idea?" The answer is simple: Trust your gut. If something sounds too good, it probably is. But if it sounds like the next big thing and you're thinking "Oh my god, this is it!" then it probably is.
